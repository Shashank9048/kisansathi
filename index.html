Key Challenges Faced by Farmers

Limited Knowledge & Training: Many farmers lack awareness of modern techniques such as protected farming (greenhouses, polyhouses) and organic cultivation.

Bureaucratic Hurdles: Complex procedures make it difficult for farmers to access government subsidies and support programs.

Farmers in India face numerous obstacles that hinder their growth and productivity. From limited access to modern techniques to bureaucratic challenges and market inefficiencies, these barriers prevent them from achieving their full potential

Market Dependence on Middlemen: Without direct market access, farmers earn lower profits.

Low Landholding & Income: Small landowners struggle with financial sustainability and need innovative, space-efficient farming techniques.

Benefits

Increased Yield: Advanced techniques like greenhouse farming and vertical farming help maximize crop production per acre.

Higher Profitability: High-value crops such as exotic fruits and organic vegetables fetch better prices in the market.

Efficient Resource Utilization: Drip irrigation and hydroponics reduce water and fertilizer wastage, making farming more sustainable.

Climate Resilience: Protected farming methods shield crops from extreme weather conditions, ensuring stable production.

Better Market Access: Direct-to-consumer sales and contract farming reduce dependency on middlemen, increasing farmers’ earnings.

Government Support & Subsidies: Farmers using modern techniques are eligible for multiple financial aids, reducing initial investment burdens.
